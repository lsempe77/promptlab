DEP Prompt Lab — Baseline Field Instructions (v2, 2026-07-07)
==============================================================

These are the v1 baseline instructions used as the starting point for each
field's prompt optimisation. The optimizer rewrites only this instruction text;
the system prompt, JSON contract, and allowed-values list are fixed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIELD: authors  (list_text)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
List EVERY author of the paper, one entry per author, in the order they appear
in the title/author block — check for co-authors named after the first author
and in footnotes, do not stop at the first name you find. Format each as
'Last name, First name Middle name' (e.g. 'Sabet, Shayda Mae'). If the paper
gives only initials for the first/middle name, keep the initials exactly as
printed (e.g. 'Miranda, J. M.') — do not guess or invent a full name you are
not certain of from the text.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIELD: author_affiliation  (list_text)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The institution(s)/organization(s) that authors are affiliated with
(university, research center, government agency, NGO). Report the PRIMARY
parent institution for each author (e.g. 'Cornell University'), NOT its
internal departments, schools, centers or programs (e.g. do not list 'Atkinson
Center for a Sustainable Future' or 'Master of Public Health Program'
separately from their parent university). List each distinct parent institution
once, using its full name with any abbreviation in brackets (e.g. 'International
Initiative for Impact Evaluation (3ie)'). Treat known name variants of the same
organization as one (e.g. 'ICDDR,B' and 'Centre for Health and Population
Research' are the same institution). If an author's affiliation is not stated
in the paper, do not invent one.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIELD: author_country  (list_categorical — 224 allowed values)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The country/countries where EACH author's institutional affiliation is located
— check every co-author's affiliation (title page, footnotes, acknowledgments),
not just the first author, and report one country per distinct institution
(there may be several if co-authors are affiliated with institutions in different
countries). If an affiliation names an organization that has country offices
worldwide (e.g. 'World Bank', 'JPAL') without specifying a particular office,
use the country of that organization's headquarters (e.g. 'JPAL' alone ->
United States; 'JPAL Africa' -> South Africa; 'World Bank' -> United States).
Use standard country names (e.g. 'United States', not 'USA' or 'US'). If a
country cannot be determined from the paper, output 'Not specified' rather than
guessing.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIELD: sector_name  (single_categorical — 11 allowed values)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Select the single World Bank sector that is the PRIMARY focus of the paper.
Papers often touch multiple sectors; pick the one that is most central to the
intervention evaluated.

Sector definitions:
- Agriculture fishing and forestry: Primary food/raw-material production —
  crop cultivation, livestock, irrigation, fisheries, forestry. NOT
  agri-business or trade.
- Education: Learning systems — early childhood, primary, secondary, tertiary,
  vocational training, adult literacy.
- Energy and extractives: Power generation, oil/gas extraction, mining, energy
  transmission and distribution.
- Financial sector: Banking, insurance, capital markets, microfinance, financial
  inclusion for the unbanked.
- Health: Medical care, disease prevention/treatment, nutrition programmes,
  maternal/child health, health system capacity — any intervention delivered
  through clinics, hospitals or health workers.
- Social protection: Cash transfers (conditional or unconditional), food
  vouchers, pensions, social insurance, safety nets, labour market programmes.
  Distinguishable from Health because the core intervention is a monetary
  transfer or entitlement, not medical care.
- Industry trade and services: Manufacturing, trade policy,
  agri-business/commercialisation, tourism, housing construction, retail and
  hospitality services.
- Information and communications technologies: Internet infrastructure, mobile
  networks, digital services.
- Public administration: Governance reform, institutional capacity, law and
  justice, decentralisation — ONLY when government management or governance
  itself is the primary subject, NOT when the paper evaluates a programme that
  happens to be administered by government.
- Transportation: Roads, railways, aviation, ports, urban transit.
- Water sanitation and waste management: Water supply, sanitation facilities,
  sewerage, solid-waste management.

Key disambiguation rules:
1. Health vs Social protection: A deworming campaign → Health. A conditional
   cash transfer requiring health check-ups → Social protection. A vitamin
   supplementation programme → Health.
2. Public administration vs any sector: A paper about reforming the health
   ministry → Health (not Public administration). A paper about fiscal
   decentralisation itself → Public administration.
3. Agriculture vs Industry/trade: Growing maize → Agriculture.
   Selling/marketing maize or agri-business → Industry trade and services.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FIELD: sub_sector  (single_categorical — 28 allowed values)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Select the most specific World Bank sub-sector using a two-step approach:

STEP 1 — Identify the main sector from the 11 options:
  Agriculture fishing and forestry | Education | Energy and extractives |
  Financial sector | Health | Social protection | Industry trade and services |
  Information and communications technologies | Public administration |
  Transportation | Water sanitation and waste management

STEP 2 — Pick the sub-sector ONLY from within that sector:

Agriculture fishing and forestry →
  Crops | Livestock | Irrigation and drainage |
  Agricultural extension, research and other support activities |
  Forestry | Fisheries |
  Public admin - Agriculture, fishing and forestry |
  Other - Agriculture, fishing and forestry

Education →
  Early childhood education | Primary education | Secondary education |
  Tertiary education | Workforce development and vocational education |
  Adult basic and continuing education |
  Public admin - Education | Other - Education

Energy and extractives →
  Mining | Oil and gas | Renewable energy - Hydro | Renewable energy - Solar |
  Renewable energy - Wind | Renewable energy - Biomass |
  Renewable energy - Geothermal | Non-renewable energy generation |
  Energy transmission and distribution |
  Public admin - Energy and extractives | Other - Energy and extractives

Financial sector →
  Banking institutions | Insurance and pension | Capital markets |
  Financial Inclusion | Other non-bank financial institutions |
  Public admin - Financial sector

Health →
  Health | Health facilities and construction | Public admin - Health

Social protection →
  Social protection | Public admin - Social protection

Industry trade and services →
  Agricultural markets, commercialization and agri-business |
  Housing contruction | Trade | Services | Manufacturing | Tourism |
  Public admin - Industry, trade and services |
  Other - Industry, trade and services

Information and communications technologies →
  ICT infrastructure | ICT services |
  Public admin - Information and communications technologies |
  Other - Information and communications technologies

Public administration →
  Law and justice | Central government or agencies |
  Sub-national government | Other - Public administration

Transportation →
  Rural and inter-urban roads | Railways | Aviation | Ports/waterways |
  Urban transportation | Public admin - Transportation | Other - Transportation

Water sanitation and waste management →
  Sanitation | Waste management | Water supply |
  Public admin - Water, sanitation and waste management |
  Other - Water, sanitation and waste management

Rules:
- Use 'Other - [Sector]' only when no specific sub-sector fits.
- Use 'Public admin - [Sector]' only when the paper is about managing the
  sector's administration, not evaluating service delivery.
